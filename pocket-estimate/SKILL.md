# Pocket Estimate

**"What's actually going on and why? What do I want to be true at the end? What's the simplest action?"**

The 7 Questions, crushed to 3. For anything that does not warrant a full estimate. Produce simple and effective plans quickly.

---

## Output Format

```
1. SITUATION:   [What is actually happening, and why does it matter? One or two sentences. Strip the noise.]
2. MISSION: [The mission either specified or implied by the users inputs.]
3. EXECUTION: [conduct mission analysis and reccomend a plan]
a. CONCEPT OF OPERATIONS:
b. MISSION STATEMENTS:
c. COORDINATING INSTRUCTIONS:
4. COMMAND AND SIGNAL:
5. COMBAT SERVICE SUPPORT:

```

---

## How to Apply

1. Take whatever the user gives you — a described problem, a messy email chain, a verbal dump.
2. Cut to the factual core: what is objectively true right now, and what is the consequence if nothing changes?
3. Identify the mission intent, list stated and implied tasks, list freedom and constraints.
4. Create three options to complete the task.Options may be alternatives of place, time, activity or a combination.
5. Analyse, based on the situation as you understand it, what could the enemy do to prevent you from achieving each option?.
6. Compare the options and indiciate the one which is most promising, propose any modifications needed to achieve the mission.
7. Compose the orders in the Output Format. this will be no more than one page. drop 4 and 5 if they arent relevant.

---

## Rules

- **Situation: two sentences maximum.** If you need more, the situation has not been understood yet — distil further.
- **Assumptions must be named.** If the situation contains an assumption (i.e. something believed true but not confirmed), flag it explicitly. Unexamined assumptions are risk.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Kill

| What you see | What's wrong | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Situation = background brief | Too long, too much context. | Two sentences: what is true now, and what is the consequence. |
