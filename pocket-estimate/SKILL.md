# Pocket Estimate

**"What's actually going on and why? What do I want to be true at the end? What's the simplest next action?"**

The 7 Questions, crushed to 3. For anything that does not warrant a full estimate. Thirty seconds of rigour, not three hours of process.

---

## Output Format

```
SITUATION:   [What is actually happening, and why does it matter? One or two sentences. Strip the noise.]
END STATE:   [What does success look like? Specific and testable.]
NEXT ACTION: [One action. One owner. One deadline.]
```

---

## How to Apply

1. Take whatever the user gives you — a described problem, a messy email chain, a verbal dump.
2. Cut to the factual core: what is objectively true right now, and what is the consequence if nothing changes?
3. Define end state: not a feeling, not a direction — a condition. Something that can be confirmed as true or false.
4. Identify the single next action that makes the most progress toward the end state with the least risk. One move, not a plan.
5. Name the owner and the deadline. If either is missing, the action is not ready.

---

## Rules

- **Situation: two sentences maximum.** If you need more, the situation has not been understood yet — distil further.
- **End state must be testable.** "Better coordination" is not an end state. "N2 and N5 are sharing product daily before 0800" is.
- **Next action is one action, one owner, one deadline.** Not a plan. Not a list. A move.
- **Assumptions must be named.** If the situation contains an assumption (i.e. something believed true but not confirmed), flag it explicitly. Unexamined assumptions are risk.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Kill

| What you see | What's wrong | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Situation = background brief | Too long, too much context. | Two sentences: what is true now, and what is the consequence. |
| End state = "improve the situation" | Not testable. | "X is true. Y is done. Z is no longer a risk." Name the condition. |
| Next action = "continue to monitor" | Not an action. | Who does what, by when, and how will you know it is done? |
| Next action = a five-step plan | Too much. The estimate finds the *next* move, not the whole route. | One step. Reassess after. |
| No owner on the next action | No owner means no action. | Name a person, not a function. |

## Examples

**Unclear tactical situation:**
```
SITUATION:   The liaison officer has not reported back and the patrol departs in two hours. Route clearance is unconfirmed.
END STATE:   The patrol commander has a confirmed go/no-go on the route before the final brief at H-90.
NEXT ACTION: OC calls the LO directly — now. If no contact in 20 minutes, assume unconfirmed and activate alternate route.
             Owner: OC. Deadline: 20 minutes from now.
```

**Staff planning problem:**
```
SITUATION:   Two annexes are missing from the orders and the O Group is in four hours. N4 and N6 have not submitted.
END STATE:   Both annexes are in the orders folder and reviewed by the COS before 1530.
NEXT ACTION: COS calls N4 and N6 now, confirms status, sets a hard submission deadline of 1400 with no extensions.
             Owner: COS. Deadline: call made within 10 minutes.
```

**Personal / command decision:**
```
SITUATION:   The CO has asked for a recommendation on two COAs by end of day and the analysis is incomplete.
END STATE:   A defensible recommendation is on the CO's desk by 1700, even if the analysis has caveated gaps.
NEXT ACTION: Planner produces a draft recommendation with explicit assumptions flagged, rather than waiting for complete data.
             Owner: SO2 Plans. Deadline: 1630 draft to COS for review.
```

---

After producing the Pocket Estimate, offer to convert the Next Action into a Task–Purpose, or to apply Main Effort if multiple actions are competing.
