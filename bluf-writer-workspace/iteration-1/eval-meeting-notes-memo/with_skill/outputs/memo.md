MEMORANDUM FOR Brigade Commander

FROM:    Col. Harris, Logistics Working Group Chair
DATE:    28 May 2026
SUBJECT: Approve Dual-Track Action to Prevent Class III Shortfall at D+8 During Exercise IRON THUNDER

1. BLUF. Exercise IRON THUNDER will exhaust pre-positioned Class III stocks at D+8 — two days short of the end state — requiring immediate approval of an emergency resupply request and a FRAGO to reduce vehicle idle time.

2. BACKGROUND. The Logistics Working Group convened 28 May 2026 to address fuel consumption rates for Exercise IRON THUNDER. Current burn rate is 140% of planned due to extended vehicle idling during waiting phases. Without intervention, stocks run dry at D+8.

3. DISCUSSION.
   a. At the current 140% burn rate, pre-positioned Class III stocks will be exhausted at D+8, two days before the exercise end state.
   b. Emergency resupply from Camp Stanton is available but costs $180K in transport and requires 72 hours advance notice — the decision window closes immediately.
   c. Revised marshalling procedures can reduce idle time by 30%, extending fuel runway to D+11, but require a FRAGO and 24 hours of driver retraining.
   d. The working group unanimously recommends executing both actions in parallel: initiating the resupply request now while implementing the FRAGO to reduce consumption.

4. RECOMMENDATION.
   a. Brigade Commander approves emergency resupply request from Camp Stanton (cost: $180K) — decision required NLT COB 28 May 2026 to meet the 72-hour lead time.
   b. S4 publishes FRAGO directing revised marshalling procedures to reduce vehicle idle time by 30%, with implementation complete within 24 hours of approval.

5. POC. Maj. Reeves, Logistics Working Group.
