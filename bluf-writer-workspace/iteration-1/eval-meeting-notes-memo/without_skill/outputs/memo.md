MEMORANDUM

TO: Brigade Commander
FROM: Logistics Working Group
SUBJECT: Class III Fuel Shortfall — Exercise IRON THUNDER — Recommended COA
DATE: 28 May 2026

**BLUF:** Exercise IRON THUNDER will exhaust pre-positioned Class III stocks by D+8 — two days short of the exercise end state. The Logistics Working Group recommends a dual approach: immediately request emergency resupply from Camp Stanton and issue a FRAGO to reduce vehicle idle time.

---

**SITUATION**

Current Class III (fuel) consumption for Exercise IRON THUNDER is running at 140% of the planned rate, primarily due to extended vehicle idling during waiting phases. At this rate, pre-positioned stocks will be exhausted at D+8, with the exercise end state at D+10.

---

**PROBLEM**

A two-day fuel gap exists between projected stock exhaustion and exercise completion. Without action, the exercise cannot be sustained through its planned end state.

---

**OPTIONS CONSIDERED**

1. **Emergency Resupply from Camp Stanton.** An emergency resupply request is available but carries a $180,000 transport cost and requires 72 hours advance notice. Decision authority must act immediately for this option to be viable.

2. **FRAGO to Reduce Idle Time.** Revised marshalling procedures could reduce idle time by 30%, extending the fuel runway to approximately D+11. Implementation requires a FRAGO and driver retraining — estimated 24 hours to take effect.

3. **Combined Approach (Recommended).** Execute both options simultaneously. The resupply covers the immediate shortfall while the FRAGO addresses the underlying consumption rate. This provides the greatest margin of safety against further slippage.

---

**RECOMMENDATION**

The Logistics Working Group unanimously recommends the combined approach: initiate the emergency resupply request from Camp Stanton immediately (to meet the 72-hour lead time) and issue a FRAGO directing revised marshalling procedures to reduce vehicle idle time by 30%.
